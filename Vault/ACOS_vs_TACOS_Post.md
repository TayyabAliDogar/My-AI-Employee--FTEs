📊 ACOS vs TACOS: Why Most Amazon Sellers Are Tracking the Wrong Metric

After optimizing $2M+ in ad spend, I've seen countless sellers obsess over ACOS while their businesses slowly die. Here's what they're missing:

**The Critical Difference:**

ACOS = Ad Spend ÷ Ad Revenue (measures ad efficiency)
TACOS = Ad Spend ÷ Total Revenue (measures business health)

One tells you if your ads work. The other tells you if your business works.

**Why TACOS Matters for Scaling:**

1. **The Halo Effect** - Your ads drive organic sales. TACOS captures this. ACOS doesn't.

2. **True Profitability** - A 25% ACOS looks great until you realize it's 40% TACOS because you have no organic sales.

3. **Growth vs Efficiency** - TACOS lets you invest in growth without panicking over short-term ACOS spikes.

**The Dangerous ACOS Misconceptions:**

❌ "Lower ACOS is always better"
Reality: A 15% ACOS with $10K revenue is worse than 30% ACOS with $100K revenue.

❌ "I need to get my ACOS under 20%"
Reality: Your target ACOS should be based on margins, not arbitrary benchmarks.

❌ "High ACOS means my campaigns are failing"
Reality: High ACOS during launch can build organic rank that drops your TACOS long-term.

**The Real Strategy:**

Optimize for TACOS, not ACOS. Accept higher ad costs if they're building organic momentum. A 35% TACOS with strong organic growth beats a 20% ACOS with no organic sales every time.

Track both metrics. Understand the relationship. Scale accordingly.
